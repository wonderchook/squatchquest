# SquatchQuest — Competitive Landscape

**Last Updated**: 2026-03-03

## Key Positioning
- Gap between expensive platforms ($400-$1,500+) and DIY chambers ($0 but staff time)
- SquatchQuest = done-for-you, $600-$1,000, small towns

## Platform Competitors
- **Goosechase** — $399-$649/event, self-service SaaS, most common in civic contexts
- **Scavify** — $1,080-$1,560/100 players, corporate-focused
- **PlayTours** — $49-$159/mo, browser-based, closest to white-label
- **Let's Roam** — ~$20/person, pre-built city tours
- **Actionbound** — ~€600/100 players, education focus
- **Watson Adventures** — $650+ full-service, metro/corporate pricing

## DIY Chambers (proving demand)
- Fruita (CO), Sedro-Woolley (WA), Little Falls (MN), Diamond City (PA), Nassau (NY), Dutchess County (NY)

## Farm Trail Comps
- Sonoma County Farm Trails (50+ years, nonprofit, $200/yr membership)
- Oregon Food Trails (13 trails, state tourism office)
- Georgia FARMeander
- French: De Ferme en Ferme (30+ years), Bienvenue à la Ferme (6,000 farms)

## Competitive Advantages
1. Price (cheaper than Goosechase + chamber doesn't do the work)
2. Done-for-you (unlike every platform)
3. No app download (browser-based)
4. Memorable Bigfoot/Sasquatch theming
5. Local PNW knowledge
6. Farm trail gamification (unique)

## Risks
1. Low barriers to entry
2. Chamber DIY if they have tech volunteers
3. Geographic scale limitations
4. Platform commoditization
